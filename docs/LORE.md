# Pond Warfare — Game World & Lore

Pond Warfare is a tower-defense RTS set in a freshwater pond ecosystem. While gameplay is mechanical and strategy-focused, the world carries character and narrative atmosphere.

## Setting

**The Pond** — A thriving wetland ecosystem, home to two rival factions competing for finite resources and dominance. The pond contains:

- Shallow marshes with cattails and lilypads
- Deep channels with rocky substrate
- Clam beds (resource nodes)
- Pearl beds (rare prestige currency)
- Floating logs and driftwood (building material)
- Submerged ruins (atmosphere)

**Time:** Eternal summer → fall transition. Matches span 30-60 minutes, with day/night cycles affecting visibility and behavior.

**Weather:** Dynamic seasonal effects — clear days, rain storms, fog banks, wind gusts that affect unit speed and visibility.

## Factions

### The Otters

**Identity:** Cooperative, resourceful, community-oriented inhabitants of the pond. Build collective lodges (lodges serve as housing, command centers, and resource drop-off points). Employ craft and teamwork.

**Units:**
- **Mudpaw** — Scrappy gatherer/infantry. Collects resources, holds the line in early combat. Backbone of the otter economy.
- **Medic** — Healer. Restores wounded allies. Fast, fragile, non-combatant role.
- **Sapper** — Combat engineer. Disables fortifications, builds structures faster. Specialist for wall breaches.
- **Saboteur** — Infiltrator. Can slip past terrain, plants temporary traps. Late-game specialist.

**Specialists (Pearl Blueprints):**
- **Fisher** — Auto-harvests nearby clams passively
- **Logger** — Auto-harvests nearby wood (twigs)
- **Digger** — Digs for hidden pearls
- **Guard** — Stationary defender, high HP, area hold
- **Ranger** — Ranged specialist with anchor and engagement zones
- **Bombardier** — Long-range attacker with splash damage
- **Shaman** — Mobile healer with terrain blessing
- **Lookout** — Scout with extended vision, flies above terrain

**Aesthetic:** Earthy tones, natural materials (driftwood, reeds, river stones), practical craftsmanship. Hand-drawn style with warm greens and browns.

### The Predators

**Identity:** Instinctive, territorial, solitary hunters driven by appetite and competition. Nest in the pond margins and stage raids to expand territory and consume resources (representing natural predation in the ecosystem).

**Units:**
- **Gator** — Crocodilian raider. Fast, mid-range melee. Primary attacker.
- **Snake** — Venomous striker. Ranged, poisonous damage over time. Skirmisher.
- **Armored Gator** — Thicker-scaled version of Gator. Slow but tanky.
- **Venom Snake** — Enhanced Snake with poison aura. Deadlier swarms.
- **Swamp Drake** — Flying predator. High vision, faster than ground units, anti-building focus.
- **Siege Turtle** — Slow, heavily armored, focuses fire on structures.

**Enemy Evolution Tiers:**
1. **Gators & Snakes** — Early scouts, weak individual units
2. **Armored variants** — Increased durability, trained tactics
3. **Drakes** — Air superiority, expanded vision
4. **Siege Turtles** — Coordinated structure assault
5. **Alpha Predator** — Hero unit, rare, devastating, ends long matches

**Aesthetic:** Cold, predatory. Scales, fangs, slimy textures. Dark greens, grays, sickly yellows. Visceral, dangerous.

## Economy & Resources

**Clams** — The primary food/currency. Otters gather clams from clam beds to feed workers, train units, and upgrade structures. Clams represent biological calories essential to sustaining activity.

**Twigs** — Building material. Harvested from fallen logs and branches. Used to construct lodges (expansion), towers (defense), walls (terrain control), and research buildings.

**Pearls** — Rare prestige gems found in deep pearl beds. Precious and hard to obtain. Spent only on elite upgrades (specialists, powerful techs). Represent long-term investment and prestige progression.

## Lodge (Home Base)

The **Lodge** is the otter collective's home — a multi-chambered structure built from logs and reeds. It serves three functions:

1. **Resource drop-off** — Gatherers bring clams and twigs here for storage
2. **Training center** — Units are trained from the Lodge
3. **Home turf** — Must defend the Lodge or lose the match

As players unlock upgrades, the Lodge visually evolves — new wings are added, decorations appear, the structure becomes more fortified. By late game, a well-upgraded Lodge looks like a sprawling fortress.

**Defense:** Walls and towers protect the lodge. Wall placement forces enemy movements into chokepoints. Towers provide automated ranged defense.

## Specialists & Autonomy

Rather than controlling every unit manually, players unlock **specialist blueprints** (Fisher, Logger, etc.) and train them to perform automated roles. Once placed, specialists patrol assigned areas, fulfill their duties (gathering, scouting, healing), and do not require direct orders.

This represents the otter civilization's ability to organize specialists into roles — division of labor. Specialists are *smarter* units that understand their responsibilities.

## Victory & Survival

**Win Condition:** Survive escalating enemy waves until all predators are defeated (or survive a timer in certain modes).

**Defeat Condition:** Lodge is destroyed or all otter units are eliminated.

The narrative arc is **survival under pressure** — starting peaceful with small raids, escalating to massive coordinated assaults from the Alpha Predator. Success requires economy, strategy, and adaptation.

## Day/Night Cycle

The pond transitions through day and night, affecting atmosphere and gameplay:

- **Day:** Full visibility, active enemies, bright colors, insect sounds
- **Dusk:** Longer shadows, cooler lighting, predators hunt more aggressively
- **Night:** Reduced visibility, fireflies emerge as ambient decoration, enemies move cautiously, audio becomes ambient/eerie
- **Dawn:** Soft light, rebirth, enemies reset to day routine

## Seasonal Progression

A match spans multiple in-game days, and the environment subtly shifts toward autumn:

- **Early**: Lush green, warm tones, summer insects
- **Mid**: Cooler light, some yellowing vegetation
- **Late**: Autumn colors, leaf debris, colder wind, approaching dormancy

This creates a sense of **time passing** and reinforces the escalating threat — winter is coming, and the predators grow more desperate.

## Combat & Roleplay

Units have personality through animation and audio:

- Mudpaws chatter when selected, display determination in attack
- Predators growl, snap, and display predatory behavior
- Medics have healing chimes
- Specialists have unique ability sounds (Shaman's blessing, Ranger's zoom)

Victory animations show otters celebrating; defeat shows retreat and resignation.

## Thematic Elements

**Cooperation vs. Predation:** Otter faction is collaborative (specialists, lodges, infrastructure), while predators are solitary and driven by instinct (no resource economy, pure combat).

**Ecosystem Balance:** The pond contains a natural food chain. Otters gather vegetarian resources; predators hunt. The game is about maintaining equilibrium or victory.

**Adaptation:** Escalating enemies force players to adapt strategy, learn enemy patterns, and evolve their army composition. Intelligence and planning beat raw power.

## Art Direction

- **Otter faction:** Warm, earthy, crafted aesthetic. Driftwood + reeds + river stone. Handmade feel.
- **Predator faction:** Cold, organic, visceral. Scales, fangs, sickly light. Dangerous, primal.
- **Environment:** Watercolor pond backgrounds, animated fog blobs, fireflies, realistic water reflections, subtle weather effects.
- **UI:** Frame9Slice panels with wooden borders and grunge texture, pond watercolor accents, serif fonts for headers (IM Fell English SC), game-width sans-serif body text.

## Narrative (if Campaign Mode Added)

Planned campaign missions (v1.5) would establish:

1. **Tutorial:** Otter Ranger trains new recruits, faces first wave
2. **Expansion:** Discovering new territory, encountering stronger predators
3. **Escalation:** Alpha Predator emerges, massive assault
4. **Alliance:** Uniting otter clans for final stand
5. **Victory:** Predators routed, peace restored (until next season)

Campaign would use voice lines, cutscenes, and cinematic dialogue to establish character and stakes.

---

**Design note:** Pond Warfare prioritizes strategy over narrative. The lore serves gameplay, not vice versa. The world should feel alive and coherent, but the game's depth comes from mechanics, not story.
